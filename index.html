Methodology — Multi-Method Valuation Model:
① P/AUM (50%): P = f × q × (1/R) × AuA — Joenväärä & Scherer (2017). Calibrated via Stratos (AuA = €450M, 5 prof. = €90M/prof). Fee rate f = Rev/AuA, margin q = FCFE/Rev.
② Peer Relative (30%): Average of (Rev × med. EV/Rev) and (EBITDA × med. EV/EBITDA). Self-referencing comp set (n=10) — Bigelli & Manuzzi (2019).
③ Normalized DCF (20%): P = Norm. FCFE / (R − g). Norm. FCFE = median of available years to smooth lumpy earnings — Damodaran (2013).
Blended EV = 0.50 × P/AUM + 0.30 × Rel. Avg + 0.20 × DCF.
Scoring: Financial 30%, Valuation 30%, Strategic Fit 30%, Responsiveness 5%, Convergence 5%.
Tier classification: Tier 1 ★ (≥ 3.50) Initiate LOI / due diligence · Tier 2 (2.50–3.49) Contact for exploratory meeting · Tier 3 (1.50–2.49) Monitor; revisit in 12 months · Tier 4 (< 1.50) No action.
Ke: 6% (large AuA), 8% (mid), 10% (small/new). g = 2.5% (Italy nominal GDP — ECB Projections 2025).
Deal structure: Roll-up with equity participation; blended prices serve as exchange ratios, not cash-out buyout figures. Est. initial cash outlay: €3–5M.
Sources: Orbis 2024, Joenväärä & Scherer (2017) JFMPM, Damodaran (2013), Moro Visconti (2020), Bigelli & Manuzzi (2019), Huberman (2006), ECB (2025).
